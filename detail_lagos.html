  1. Forte da Ponta da Bandeira

    Forte da Ponta da Bandeira is a historic fortress located at the entrance to the harbor of Lagos, in the Algarve region of Portugal. Built in the 17th century, it was originally constructed to protect the town from pirate attacks and enemy ships. The fortress features bastions, a drawbridge, and defensive walls, offering impressive views of the coastline. Today, it houses a museum that displays exhibits on the military history of the region, as well as maritime artifacts. The fortress is a popular landmark for visitors interested in Lagos' history and coastal defense.

  2. Sagres Fortress

    Sagres Fortress (Fortaleza de Sagres) is a historic military fort located on the southwestern tip of the Algarve, Portugal. Built in the 15th century, it is associated with the Portuguese Age of Exploration, as it was a key location for Prince Henry the Navigator and his maritime expeditions. The fortress offers stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean and features impressive walls, bastions, and a lighthouse. Visitors can explore the site, which includes remnants of old buildings, a vast central courtyard, and the Compass Rose, a large stone circle once used for navigation. It is a fascinating destination for history enthusiasts and those interested in maritime exploration.

    9:30-17:30 Recommended sightseeing time:1.5-2 hours 8650-360 Sagres, Portugal

  3. Silves Castle

    Silves Castle (Castelo de Silves) is a stunning medieval fortress located in the town of Silves, in the Algarve region of Portugal. Originally built by the Moors in the 11th century, the castle was later expanded and fortified by the Portuguese. The castle is known for its impressive red sandstone walls, towers, and battlements, which offer panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. Visitors can explore the interior courtyard, remains of the cistern, and the archaeological museum that showcases artifacts from different periods of the region’s history. Silves Castle is a must-visit for those interested in medieval architecture and Moorish heritage.

    24hours Recommended sightseeing time:1 hour R. do Castelo, 8300-135 Silves, Portugal

  4. Fortaleza de Santa Catarina

    Fortaleza de Santa Catarina is a historic fortress located in Figueira da Foz, Portugal, on a hilltop with panoramic views of the Atlantic Ocean and the surrounding area. Built in the 16th century, the fortress was initially constructed to protect the region from pirate attacks and naval threats. It features bastions, cannon emplacements, and defensive walls. Today, the fortress is a popular tourist attraction, offering visitors a glimpse into the region's military history. It also provides a stunning setting for walking and sightseeing. The fortress is a peaceful and scenic spot, ideal for those interested in history and coastal views.

  1. Centro Ciência Viva de Lagos

    The Centro Ciência Viva de Lagos is a science center located in the town of Lagos, in the Algarve region of Portugal. The center focuses on oceanography, maritime history, and local ecosystems, with interactive exhibits and educational displays. It highlights the region's connection to the sea, featuring exhibits on marine life, navigation, and the environment. The center offers hands-on activities, workshops, and a planetarium, making it an engaging experience for all ages. Visitors can learn about the importance of the ocean and Lagos' role in Portugal's maritime history. It's a great destination for science enthusiasts and families.

  2. Museu Municipal Dr. Jose Formosinho

    The Museu Municipal Dr. José Formosinho is a municipal museum located in Lagos, Portugal. Housed in a historic building, the museum offers a rich collection of artifacts related to the region's history, culture, and art. It features displays on archaeology, religious art, ethnography, and local history, including items from the Roman and Moorish periods. One of the highlights of the museum is its collection of religious sculptures and ceramics. The museum provides a fascinating insight into the history of Lagos and the Algarve, making it a must-visit for history and culture enthusiasts.

    10:00-13:00, 14:00-18:00 Recommended sightseeing time:1 hour R. Gen. Alberto da Silveira s/n, 8600-594 Lagos, Portugal

  3. Laboratorio de Actividades Criativas

    Laboratório de Atividades Criativas is a creative workshop space located in Lagos, Portugal. It is designed to encourage artistic expression and creativity through a variety of hands-on activities. The lab offers workshops in areas like painting, sculpture, photography, and crafts, providing both locals and tourists with the opportunity to explore their artistic talents. It also hosts educational programs for children and adults, fostering a community of creativity. The space aims to inspire and promote artistic development and cultural exchange in a welcoming environment.

  1. Praia do Camilo

    Praiado Camilo is a well-known beach in Lagos, Portugal. The water is clear and the sand is of good texture. There are white sandy beaches, steep rock cliffs, and small fishing towns with bright colors. The scenery is very beautiful.

  2. Praia de Dona Ana

    Praia de Dona Ana is one of the most beautiful beaches in the Algarve, located near Lagos, Portugal. Known for its stunning golden sand and crystal-clear waters, the beach is surrounded by impressive rock formations and cliffs, creating a picturesque setting. It is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and exploring the caves and grottos along the coastline. The beach is easily accessible and offers amenities like restaurants and cafes. With its breathtaking natural beauty, Praia de Dona Ana is often considered one of the top beaches in Portugal.

    24hours Recommended sightseeing time:1-2 hours 8600-315 Lagos, Portugal

  3. Pinhao Beach

    Pinhão Beach is a beautiful and relatively quieter beach located in the Algarve, near the town of Aljezur in Portugal. Known for its natural beauty, the beach is surrounded by cliffs and offers stunning views of the coastline. The clear, turquoise waters are perfect for swimming and the beach is often less crowded compared to others in the region, making it ideal for those seeking a more peaceful and relaxing environment. The beach is accessible via a scenic walk down the cliffs, offering a tranquil retreat with a more untouched, natural feel.
