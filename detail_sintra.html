Sintra/Cascais

  1. National Palace of Pena

    Pena Palace, located in Sintra near Lisbon, is a striking blend of Gothic, Renaissance, Moorish, and Manueline architectural styles. Originally a chapel, it became a royal residence after King Manuel I built a monastery in the 15th century. Destroyed in the 1755 Lisbon earthquake, it was later transformed into a royal summer palace by Ferdinand of Saxe-Coburg-Gotha, husband of Queen Maria II, and completed in 1885. A UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1995, it is now used for state activities and remains one of Portugal's seven wonders. The palace's colorful, unique design makes it look like a fairy tale castle.

    09:30-18:30(Tickets available until 17:30; admission ends at 18:00) Recommended sightseeing time:2-3 hours Estrada da Pena, 2710-609 Sintra, Portugal

  2. Quinta da Regaleira

    The Regaleira Estate, located near Sintra's historic center, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a major tourist attraction. The estate features the romantic Regaleira Palace, Regaleira Chapel, and an expansive park filled with lakes, caves, wells, fountains, and unique buildings. Known as the "Millionaire Monteiro's Palace" after its first owner, the estate includes a 27-meter deep well surrounded by a spiral staircase with 9 layers, symbolizing the cycles of heaven, hell, and purgatory. Visitors can explore the well and surrounding garden, creating a magical and immersive experience.

    10:00-17:30 Recommended sightseeing time:1.5-2.5 hours Rua Barbosa do Bocage, Sintra 2710-567, Portugal
    see more

  3. Cape Roca

    Cape Roca, located about 40 kilometers from Lisbon in the Sintra-Cascais Natural Park, is the westernmost point of Portugal and once thought to be the edge of the world. This dramatic cliff, rising 140 meters above the Atlantic Ocean, offers breathtaking views of the vast sea and sky. The steep cliffs, resembling outstretched arms, create a stunning, almost surreal landscape. Visitors can walk along the cliffs and enjoy magnificent sunrises and sunsets. Guardrails are installed to ensure safety while exploring this majestic natural landmark.

    24hours Recommended sightseeing time:1-2 hours Estrada do Cabo da Roca s/n, 2705-001 Colares
    see more

  4. Sintra National Palace

    The Royal Palace of Sintra, located near Lisbon, was built by King John I and completed during King Manuel I's reign in the 16th century. It is one of Portugal's best-preserved medieval royal palaces and was home to the royal family from the 15th to 19th centuries. The palace blends Moorish and Visigothic architectural styles, with its distinctive twin white chimneys earning it the nickname "Horn Palace." Key rooms include the Swan Hall, featuring 27 swans on the ceiling, the Mermaid Room, China Room, and Badge Room. As part of Sintra's cultural landscape, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

    09:30-18:30(Admission ends at 18:00) Recommended sightseeing time:1-2 hours Largo Rainha Dona Amélia, 2710-616 Sintra, Portugal
    see more

  5. Moorish Castle

    The Moorish Castle, located on a hilltop in Sintra, is a 17th-century Moorish-style castle and a prominent European landmark. It was designated a Portuguese national monument in 1910 and is a popular tourist destination. Despite parts of the castle being collapsed, its 300-meter city wall offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. The castle’s layout, reminiscent of the Great Wall of China with its twists and bunkers, stretches across the Sintra Mountains. From the castle's summit, visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the Atlantic Ocean, Sintra, and on clear days, Ericeira and Mafra.

    09:30-18:00(Tickets available until 17:30; admission ends at 17:30) Recommended sightseeing time:3-5 hours 2710-405 Sintra, Portugal

  1. Air Museum

    The Air Museum (Museu do Ar) in Portugal is the country's main aviation museum, located in Sintra, near Lisbon. It showcases a vast collection of aircraft, engines, uniforms, and aviation-related artifacts. The museum highlights Portugal’s aviation history, including military and civil aviation. Visitors can see iconic planes like the DC-3, T-6, and the first aircraft to cross the South Atlantic. It offers an immersive experience for aviation enthusiasts and history lovers.

    10:00-17:00 Recommended sightseeing time:1 hour 2715-021 Pêro Pinheiro, Portugal
    see more

  2. Museu das Artes de Sintra

    The Museu das Artes de Sintra (Museum of the Arts of Sintra) is a cultural space in Sintra, Portugal, dedicated to visual arts. It features a diverse collection of contemporary and classical artworks from Portuguese and international artists. The museum is housed in a historic building, adding to its charm and artistic atmosphere. It regularly hosts temporary exhibitions, workshops, and cultural events. Visitors can explore paintings, sculptures, and other artistic expressions in a picturesque setting.

    10:00-18:00 Recommended sightseeing time:1 hour Av. Heliodoro Salgado SN, 2710-575 Sintra, Portugal
    see more

  3. NewsMuseum

    The NewsMuseum in Sintra, Portugal, is an interactive museum dedicated to journalism, media, and communication. It explores the impact of news, propaganda, and social media on society through multimedia exhibits. Visitors can experience historic news events, try being a TV anchor, and learn about press freedom. The museum covers topics like war reporting, fake news, and the digital revolution. It offers an engaging and educational experience for media enthusiasts and history lovers.

    10:00-18:30 Recommended sightseeing time:1 hour R. Visc. de Monserrate 26, 2710-591 Sintra, Portugal
    see more

  4. Museu Nacional de Arte Antiga

    Located in Lisbon's wealthy diplomatic district, the Museum of Ancient Art was created in 1884 and renovated in 1911, and the museum's collection integrates a collection of existing national works. There are various fine and decorative arts from the twelfth to the nineteenth centuries, giving an impressive presentation of Portugal. The museum houses masterpieces by European and Asian artists, such as the "Belém Monstrance", as well as other works of art documenting Portuguese heritage worldwide, such as the "Nanban Screen" and so on.

    10:00-18:00 Recommended sightseeing time:1-2 hours R. das Janelas Verdes, 1249-017 Lisbon, Portugal
    see more

  5. Museu of Historia Natural

    The Museu de História Natural de Sintra (Natural History Museum of Sintra) is a museum dedicated to the natural sciences. Located in the historic center of Sintra, Portugal, it showcases a vast collection of fossils, minerals, and taxidermy specimens. The exhibits include rare dinosaur fossils and prehistoric artifacts, offering insights into Earth’s history. The museum also features interactive displays for an educational experience. It is a great destination for science lovers and families.

    10:00-18:00 Recommended sightseeing time:1 hour Rua do Paco 20 | São Martinho, Sintra 2710-602, Portugal

    There are many sailboats in Cascais Marina, which is perfect for walking and enjoying the sea view, and it is a good place to relax. There are many high-end restaurants, bars and luxury villas around the seaside, yachts, castle museums, etc. are all located nearby.

    08:30-02:00 Recommended sightseeing time:0.5-1 day Cascais 2750-800, Portugal

  2. Praia da Conceicao

    Praia da Conceição is a popular beach in Cascais, Portugal, known for its golden sand and calm waters. It is located near the town center, making it easily accessible for visitors. The beach is ideal for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports like paddleboarding. Surrounded by restaurants and cafés, it offers a vibrant atmosphere. With scenic coastal views, it is a favorite spot for both locals and tourists.
