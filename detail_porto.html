Porto

  1. Ribeira

    The old street along the Douro River in Porto is a historic area, now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The river, central to Porto’s charm, has played a key role in the city's history and culture. Centuries ago, Porto wines were shipped from here, helping establish its famous wine industry. Visitors can explore the traditional buildings, enjoy the riverside ambiance, and take in the city's rich heritage. At sunset, sipping a glass of port wine while admiring the Douro River offers a truly unforgettable experience.

    24hours Recommended sightseeing time:2-3 hours Ribeira, Porto, Portugal

  2. Clerigos Tower

    This iconic building in Porto was built in 1732 and completed only 30 years ago. It is a typical Baroque tower. Standing on the commanding heights here, the roofs of the residential buildings in front of you are full of bright colors like autumn leaves, adding a jumping vitality to the old city. When the sun goes down, the forest-like scenery will make you feel like a dream.

    09:00-19:00(Admission ends at 18:30) Recommended sightseeing time:1 hour Rua Sao Filipe Neri, Porto Portugal
    see more

  3. Bolsa Palace

    It's easy to mistake oneself for being in a palace in the stock exchange market built by the Porto Chamber of Commerce in 1842. This neoclassical building in the center of the old city has an obvious purpose to attract European investors from all over. The interior decoration is beautiful and combines the early styles of many countries.

    9:00-18:30 Recommended sightseeing time:0.5-1 hour R. de Ferreira Borges, 4050-253 Porto, Portugal
    see more

  4. Crystal Palace

    The Crystal Palace in Porto was originally built in 1865 for an industrial and agricultural exhibition, inspired by London’s Crystal Palace. Designed by José Carlos Lorero, its steel-framed glass structure gave it a modern look. Demolished in 1951, it was later rebuilt on the same site, retaining its original name. Now surrounded by gardens, it serves as a venue for sports events and trade fairs, accommodating up to 5,000 people. Nearby, visitors can enjoy fountains, libraries, performance halls, and cafes.

    08:00-19:00 Recommended sightseeing time:2-3 hours Rua de D. Manuel II, 4050-346 Porto

  5. Sandema

    The wine cellar was founded in 1790 and is a well-known wine cellar in Portugal. There are guides here who will explain the brewing process of Porto wine, and you can also taste different flavors of Porto wine, which is very worth visiting.

  1. Wine Museum

    The Wine Museum in Porto, located on the Douro River's scenic banks, offers an interactive experience to explore the history and production of Porto’s famous Port wine. Housed in a 17th-century building, the museum uses engaging video materials to complement its exhibits, making the history of wine both informative and enjoyable. Visitors can learn about the development and commercialization of Port wine while enjoying tastings of this local specialty. The museum provides a memorable experience that combines education with the opportunity to sample Porto's iconic wines.

    10:00-17:30 Recommended sightseeing time:2-3 hours Rua da Reboleira 37, 4050-492 Porto
    see more

  2. Museu Serralves

    The Seravis Pavilion blends museums and parks to create a distinctive architectural environment. Inspired by the bow-shaped windows of the museum, its unique hexagonal design is repeated in the surrounding park, pavement, and gardens. The building’s concave polygonal layout harmonizes with the natural landscape, gradually revealing a changing appearance as the environment shifts. This architectural design allows the pavilion to blend seamlessly into its surroundings, offering a fresh perspective over time.

    10:00-18:00 Recommended sightseeing time:2-3 hours R. Dom João de Castro 210, 4150-417 Porto, Portugal
    see more

  3. FC Porto Museum

    The FC Porto Museum is a museum dedicated to the history and achievements of FC Porto, one of Portugal's most successful football clubs. Located at Estádio do Dragão in Porto, it showcases trophies, memorabilia, and interactive exhibits. The museum highlights key moments, legendary players, and the club's national and international victories. Visitors can experience a multimedia journey through FC Porto's legacy, including a 360° audiovisual room. It is a must-visit destination for football fans and those interested in Portuguese sports history.

    14:30-19:00 Recommended sightseeing time:2-3 hours Via Futebol Clube do Porto - Estádio do Dragão, 4350-415 Porto, Portugal
    see more

  4. World of Discoveries

    The World of Discoveries is an interactive museum and theme park in Porto, Portugal, dedicated to the Age of Exploration. It recreates the journeys of Portuguese navigators who discovered new lands in the 15th and 16th centuries. Visitors can experience life aboard caravels, explore exotic destinations, and engage with historical exhibits. The museum combines technology, replicas, and immersive experiences to bring history to life. It is an educational and entertaining attraction for all ages.

    10:00-19:00 Recommended sightseeing time:2-3 hours Rua de Miragaia 106, 4050-387 Porto, Portugal
    see more

  5. National Museum Soares dos Reis

    The National Museum Soares dos Reis in Porto is one of Portugal’s oldest museums, founded in 1833. It is housed in the historic Palácio dos Carrancas and showcases a rich collection of 19th-century Portuguese art. The museum features paintings, sculptures, ceramics, glassware, and jewelry, with works by renowned artists like Soares dos Reis and Silva Porto. Visitors can explore the evolution of Portuguese art and culture in an elegant setting. It is a must-visit destination for art and history enthusiasts.

    10:00-18:00 Recommended sightseeing time:2-3 hours Rua D. Manuel II 44, 4050-342 Porto
    see more

  1. River Douro

    The River Douro is one of the longest rivers in the Iberian Peninsula, flowing from Spain to Portugal before reaching the Atlantic Ocean in Porto. It is famous for its scenic landscapes, vineyards, and the production of Port wine in the Douro Valley, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The river plays a crucial role in Portugal’s culture and economy, supporting agriculture, tourism, and transportation. Visitors can enjoy river cruises, explore historic towns, and experience breathtaking views of terraced vineyards. The Douro River is a symbol of Portugal’s natural beauty and rich heritage.

    24hours Recommended sightseeing time:1-3 hours Porto | Arrabida, Porto, Portugal
    see more

  2. Jardim Botanico do Porto

    The Jardim Botânico do Porto is a beautiful botanical garden in Porto, Portugal, managed by the University of Porto. It features diverse plant species, including exotic trees, vibrant flowers, and themed gardens like the Rose Garden and Succulent Garden. The garden also has a historical connection, as it was once the home of the Andresen family, including famous Portuguese writer Sophia de Mello Breyner Andresen. Visitors can enjoy peaceful walks, explore greenhouses, and observe local wildlife. It is a perfect spot for nature lovers, students, and those seeking tranquility in the city.

    10:00-13:00, 14:00-18:00 Recommended sightseeing time:1-3 hours Rua do Campo Alegre 1191, 4150-181 Porto, Portugal
    see more

  3. Praia da Luz

    Praia da Luz is a beautiful beach located in Porto, Portugal, offering stunning ocean views and a relaxing atmosphere. It is known for its golden sands, rocky cliffs, and the famous Praia da Luz restaurant, which provides delicious seafood and breathtaking sunset views. The beach is a popular spot for both locals and tourists to enjoy a peaceful escape from the city. Visitors can take a stroll along the coast, sunbathe, or enjoy a meal with an oceanfront view. It’s an ideal place for a laid-back and scenic experience in Porto.

    09:00–02:00 (the next day) Recommended sightseeing time:2-3 hours R. Cel. Raúl Peres, 4150-155 Porto, Portugal
    see more

  4. Quinta do Covelo

    Quinta do Covelo is a charming urban park in Porto, Portugal, offering green spaces, playgrounds, and walking paths. Once a private estate, it has been transformed into a public park, providing a peaceful retreat from the city’s busy streets. The park features a children’s play area, picnic spots, and shaded trails, making it perfect for families and nature lovers. It also hosts environmental education programs and activities for the community. Quinta do Covelo is an excellent spot for relaxation, outdoor exercise, and enjoying nature in Porto.

    7:00-20:00 Recommended sightseeing time:2-3 hours Parque Covelo, R. de Bolama, 4200-042 Porto, Portugal
    see more

  5. Parque de Sao Roque

    Parque de São Roque is a peaceful and picturesque park in Porto, Portugal, known for its beautiful gardens and scenic views. The park features walking paths, lush greenery, and a charming labyrinth garden, making it a unique spot for visitors. Originally part of a noble estate, it retains a historical charm with statues, fountains, and traditional Portuguese landscaping. It is a great place for relaxation, picnics, and family outings away from the city's hustle and bustle. Parque de São Roque is a hidden gem for nature lovers in Porto.

    8:00-20:00 Recommended sightseeing time:2-3 hours R. São Roque da Lameira 2040, 4350-307 Porto, Portugal
