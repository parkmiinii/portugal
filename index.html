National Palace of Pena

Pena Palace, located in Sintra near Lisbon, is a striking blend of Gothic, Renaissance, Moorish, and Manueline architectural styles. Originally a chapel, it became a royal residence after King Manuel I built a monastery in the 15th century. Destroyed in the 1755 Lisbon earthquake, it was later transformed into a royal summer palace by Ferdinand of Saxe-Coburg-Gotha, husband of Queen Maria II, and completed in 1885. A UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1995, it is now used for state activities and remains one of Portugal's seven wonders. The palace's colorful, unique design makes it look like a fairy tale castle.

09:30-18:30(Tickets available until 17:30, admission ends at 18:00) Recommended sightseeing time:2-3 hours Estrada da Pena, 2710-609 Sintra, Portugal

Belém Tower

Belém Tower is a historic landmark in Lisbon, Portugal, built between 1514-1520 by Manuel I and designated a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1983. It served as a fortress, lighthouse, and customs house, symbolizing Portugal's Age of Exploration. The tower, made of white marble, features Manueline-style stone carvings, including maritime motifs and a striking rhinoceros sculpture. From the top platform, visitors can enjoy stunning views of the Tagus River, Jerónimos Monastery, and the April 25 Bridge. Due to strong winds at the top, visitors should dress accordingly for protection and warmth.

10:00-17:30(Admission ends at 17:00) Recommended sightseeing time:1-2 hours Av. Brasilia, 1400-038 Lisbon

Jerónimos Monastery

The Jerónimos Monastery, located in Lisbon’s Belém district, is a grand architectural masterpiece and a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1983. Built in 1501 by King Manuel I to honor Vasco da Gama’s voyage, it took nearly 100 years to complete. The monastery features intricate Manueline-style carvings, a magnificent south gate, and a vast Gothic nave with a high vaulted ceiling. Inside, Da Gama’s tomb and the poet Camões’ sarcophagus highlight Portugal’s maritime and literary legacy. The cloister offers stunning views of the courtyard, showcasing the elegance of Portuguese architecture.

9:30-18:00(Admission ends at 17:30) Recommended sightseeing time:1-1.5 hours Praca do Imperio Belém, Lisbon 1400-206 Portugal

Livraria Lello

Lello Bookstore is one of the oldest bookstores in Porto, Portugal. Its neo-Gothic façade and staircase were designed and built by architect Estevez. The top floor of the bookstore has food and wine, and books in Portuguese, English and French are everywhere. Since its opening in 1881, Lello Bookstore has attracted a large number of readers with its good design. There is a beautiful wooden staircase in the center of the bookstore, which leads to the roof. Readers can look up at the sky 360 degrees through the glass roof. The bookshelves around the stairs are filled with all kinds of good books, making the "bookworms" linger.

09:00-19:30 Recommended sightseeing time:0.5-1 hour R. das Carmelitas 144, 4050-161 Porto, Portugal

Quinta da Regaleira

The Regaleira Estate, located near Sintra's historic center, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a major tourist attraction. The estate features the romantic Regaleira Palace, Regaleira Chapel, and an expansive park filled with lakes, caves, wells, fountains, and unique buildings. Known as the "Millionaire Monteiro's Palace" after its first owner, the estate includes a 27-meter deep well surrounded by a spiral staircase with 9 layers, symbolizing the cycles of heaven, hell, and purgatory. Visitors can explore the well and surrounding garden, creating a magical and immersive experience.

10:00-17:30 Recommended sightseeing time:1.5-2.5 hours Rua Barbosa do Bocage, Sintra 2710-567, Portugal

Alfama

Alfama Old Town is an ancient area of Lisbon with many historical monuments. It is located on a small hill in the Tejo Bay. The hillsides here are undulating, there are countless winding stone steps and alleys, and the old houses have strong colored outer walls, exuding a retro charm everywhere. There are many grocery stores on both sides of the streets in the old city that maintain the same appearance as they used to be, full of food and daily necessities. There are many bars and cafés, and it is the ideal place for Lisbon's nightlife.

24hours Recommended sightseeing time:0.5-1 day Lisbon, Portugal

São Jorge Castle

São Jorge Castle is one of the oldest buildings in Lisbon and is the main historical monument and tourist attraction in Lisbon. The castle was built on the high point of the Alfama region and was the fortress when the Moors occupied Lisbon in the Middle Ages. Because it is located on a commanding hillside, it can be seen from almost every corner of Lisbon. The castle originally belonged to the earlier Visigoths. After the Moors invaded, it underwent a large-scale expansion. The 8-meter-thick city wall still makes people feel extraordinary. Here one can better appreciate the city's architectural art, whether it's the futuristic castle and drawbridge from Alfama to the city in the distance, or the open-air ruins of the Carmo church in the city.

09:00-18:00(Admission ends at 17:30) Recommended sightseeing time:1-2 hours Castle of S. Jorge, Rua de Santa Cruz, 1100-129 Lisbon

Cape Roca

Cape Roca, located about 40 kilometers from Lisbon in the Sintra-Cascais Natural Park, is the westernmost point of Portugal and once thought to be the edge of the world. This dramatic cliff, rising 140 meters above the Atlantic Ocean, offers breathtaking views of the vast sea and sky. The steep cliffs, resembling outstretched arms, create a stunning, almost surreal landscape. Visitors can walk along the cliffs and enjoy magnificent sunrises and sunsets. Guardrails are installed to ensure safety while exploring this majestic natural landmark.

24hours Recommended sightseeing time:1-2 hours Estrada do Cabo da Roca s/n, 2705-001 Colares

Sintra National Palace

The Royal Palace of Sintra, located near Lisbon, was built by King John I and completed during King Manuel I's reign in the 16th century. It is one of Portugal's best-preserved medieval royal palaces and was home to the royal family from the 15th to 19th centuries. The palace blends Moorish and Visigothic architectural styles, with its distinctive twin white chimneys earning it the nickname "Horn Palace." Key rooms include the Swan Hall, featuring 27 swans on the ceiling, the Mermaid Room, China Room, and Badge Room. As part of Sintra's cultural landscape, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

09:30-18:30(Admission ends at 18:00) Recommended sightseeing time:1-2 hours Largo Rainha Dona Amélia, 2710-616 Sintra, Portugal

Monument to the Discoveries

The Nautical Monument, also called the Great Discovery Monument, was inaugurated in 1960 in Lisbon. It commemorates the 500th anniversary of Prince Henry the Navigator’s death. Designed like a white stone sailboat, it features intricate carvings of Henry, da Gama, and 80 other sailors, generals, missionaries, and scientists. The 52-meter-high monument has 7 floors with exhibition rooms and a viewing platform. Visitors can explore nautical exhibits and enjoy panoramic views of the city and harbor.

10:00-19:00 Recommended sightseeing time:5-15 minutes Av. Brasília 1400-038 Lisbon
