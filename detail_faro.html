Faro

  1. Arco da Vila

    The Town Arch is one of Faro's landmarks, located diagonally opposite the Church of Mercy. The arch is built on the remaining Arab city wall, which used to be the north gate of the old city of Faro. The arch successfully integrates Arabic, Moorish and Italian elements and is very suitable for taking pictures.

    24hours Recommended sightseeing time:20-30 minutes Rua da Misericordia 8 Centro Histórico, Faro 8000-269 Portugal

  2. Roman Ruins of Milreu

    The Roman Ruins of Milreu are an important archaeological site in southern Portugal, near Estoi in the Algarve region. Dating back to the 1st century AD, they include remains of a luxurious Roman villa, baths, a temple, and intricate mosaics. The site provides insights into Roman life, featuring well-preserved fish-themed mosaic floors. It was later adapted for use in the Visigothic and Islamic periods. Today, it is a fascinating destination for history and archaeology enthusiasts.

    9:00-17:00 Recommended sightseeing time:1 hour 8005-411 Estoi, Portugal
    see more

  3. The Old Town

    Faro Old Town (Cidade Velha de Faro) is the historic center of Faro, Portugal, enclosed by ancient city walls. It features cobbled streets, charming whitewashed buildings, and landmarks like the Arco da Vila and Sé de Faro (Faro Cathedral). The area reflects Moorish and medieval influences, offering a glimpse into the city’s rich history. Visitors can explore small cafés, museums, and scenic viewpoints overlooking the Ria Formosa lagoon. It’s a must-visit for those interested in history and culture.

    24hours Recommended sightseeing time:1-2 hours Faro Portugal
    see more

  4. Arco do Repouso

    The Arco do Repouso (Arch of Rest) is a historic gateway in Faro Old Town, Portugal. It is part of the medieval city walls and is believed to have been built during the Moorish period. The arch's name, meaning "Arch of Rest," comes from a legend that King Afonso III rested there after conquering Faro in 1249. It showcases a blend of Moorish and Gothic architectural influences. Today, it remains an important cultural and historical landmark in Faro.

    24hours Recommended sightseeing time:1-2 hours R. do Repouso 3, 8000-169 Faro, Portugal

  1. Museu Regional do Algarve

    The Museu Regional do Algarve is a museum in Faro, Portugal, dedicated to the history and culture of the Algarve region. It showcases traditional Algarvian crafts, tools, furniture, and clothing, offering insight into local life in the past. The museum features paintings, photographs, and dioramas depicting rural and fishing communities. It provides a glimpse into the region’s rich heritage, from agriculture to maritime traditions. A visit here is perfect for those interested in Algarve’s cultural history.

    10:00-18:00 Recommended sightseeing time:1-2 hours Praca da Liberdade 2, Faro 8000-164 Portugal
    see more

  2. Museu de Portimao

    The Museu de Portimão is a museum located in the town of Portimão in the Algarve region of Portugal. Housed in a former sardine canning factory, the museum highlights the industrial history of the area, particularly the sardine fishing industry that once thrived there. Exhibits include artifacts, tools, and machinery related to the canning process, as well as displays about Portimão’s maritime history and local archaeology. The museum also showcases contemporary art and hosts various cultural events, making it an engaging destination for history and art enthusiasts.

    10:00-18:00 Recommended sightseeing time:1-2 hours Rua D. Carlos I Zona Ribeirinha, 8500-607 Portimão, Portugal
    see more

  3. Castelo De Loule

    Castelo de Loulé is a historic castle located in the town of Loulé, in the Algarve region of Portugal. Dating back to the 13th century, it was originally built by the Moors and later expanded by the Portuguese. The castle features a mix of Moorish and medieval architectural styles, with its distinctive battlements and towers. The site also includes a beautiful courtyard and panoramic views of Loulé and the surrounding area. Today, the castle houses a local history museum, showcasing the culture and heritage of the region. It is a must-see for history lovers and visitors exploring Loulé.

    10:00-13:30, 14:30-18:00 Recommended sightseeing time:1-2 hours R. Dom Paio Peres Correia 17, 8100-564 Loulé, Portugal

  1. Praia de Faro

    Faro Beach is located 6 kilometers southwest of Faro city. The beach stretches for dozens of kilometers and has a very wide field of vision. The extension of the beach is located on the island of Cape Santa Maria on the southern tip of mainland Portugal and is one of the better natural beaches in Europe. In the evening, some people hold parties here, singing and dancing are very lively.

  2. Barreta Island

    The Isle of Desolation is located just south of Faro and is an island at the southern tip of mainland Portugal. There are fewer tourists here than Faro Beach to the northwest, and the surrounding area is very peaceful. The southern part of the entire island is a golden sandy beach, where you can enjoy the sea breeze, which is very comfortable.
