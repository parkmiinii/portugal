    Santiago Castle is located at the end of Art Painted Street. It is an old yellow castle with a long history. There are several expensive classic cars in the castle to visit, and now there is a high-end restaurant and museum in the castle, which is also one of the landmarks of Funchal.

  2. Igreja de Nossa Senhora do Monte

    Igreja de Nossa Senhora do Monte is a beautiful church located in Funchal, on the island of Madeira, Portugal. Perched on a hilltop, it offers stunning views over the city and surrounding landscapes. The church, built in the 15th century, is dedicated to Our Lady of the Mountain (Nossa Senhora do Monte), and it holds historical significance as a place of pilgrimage. The interior of the church features beautiful tilework, religious art, and an altar dedicated to the Virgin Mary. The church is also famous for its connection to the last resting place of Prince Henry the Navigator. Visitors can enjoy its serene atmosphere and panoramic views of Funchal.

    24hours Recommended sightseeing time:1-2 hours Rampa da Sacristia 1, 9050-285 Funchal, Portugal

  3. Quinta Vigia

    Quinta Vigia is a historic building and palace located in Funchal, Madeira, Portugal. Originally built in the 18th century, it has been used for various purposes over the years, including as a private residence and a military building. Today, it serves as the official residence of the President of the Regional Government of Madeira. The estate features beautiful gardens and offers scenic views of the surrounding area. Visitors can admire the lush, well-maintained grounds, although the building itself is not typically open to the public. Quinta Vigia remains an important part of Madeira’s heritage and a symbol of the island's history.

    9:00-17:00 Recommended sightseeing time:1 hour Avenida Infante, Funchal, Madeira 9000-015, Portugal
    see more

  4. Varadouros Old Gate (City Gates)

    Varadouros Old Gate (Porta de Varadouros) is one of the historic city gates in Funchal, Madeira, Portugal. This gate was once part of the town's defensive walls, which were built to protect the city from potential invasions. Located in the old town (Zona Velha), it offers a glimpse into the medieval architecture and history of Funchal. The gate itself is an example of Portuguese colonial architecture and is an important part of the city’s historical legacy. Visitors can explore the area surrounding the gate, which is rich in charm and history, reflecting Funchal's evolution over the centuries.

    24hours Recommended sightseeing time:10 minutes Largo dos Varadouros 4, 9000-503 Funchal, Portugal

  1. CR7 Cristiano Ronaldo Museum

    CR7Museum, is a collection of Cristiano Ronaldo trophies. Above the museum is a hotel built by Ronaldo and the Pestana Group. The floor of the museum is in the style of the Portuguese sidewalk and is decorated with the museum's logo. The museum is the official sponsor of the local football team União da Madeira.

  2. Madeira Story Centre

    The Madeira Story Centre is an interactive museum located in Funchal, Madeira, Portugal. It offers an immersive experience that takes visitors through the history, culture, and traditions of Madeira Island. The museum features engaging exhibits that cover key aspects of the island's past, from its discovery in the 15th century to the development of its economy, agriculture, and cultural heritage. The center includes multimedia displays, models, and historical artifacts, providing a comprehensive look at the island's unique identity. It's a great place for visitors to learn more about Madeira’s rich history in a fun and educational way.

    10:00-19:00 Recommended sightseeing time:2-3 hours R. Dom Carlos I 27, 9060-051 Funchal, Portugal
    see more

  3. Design Centre Nini Andrade Silva

    The Design Centre Nini Andrade Silva is a museum and exhibition space located in Funchal, Madeira, dedicated to the work of renowned Portuguese designer Nini Andrade Silva. The center showcases the designer’s unique approach to interior design, architecture, and art, with a focus on Madeira’s natural beauty and cultural heritage. It features a variety of exhibits, including furniture, textiles, and design concepts that reflect Silva’s creative vision. The space offers visitors the opportunity to explore cutting-edge design and innovation, making it a must-visit for design enthusiasts and those interested in contemporary art and architecture.

    11:00-23:00 Recommended sightseeing time:10 minutes Estrada da Pontinha, Forte de Nossa Senhora da Conceição, 9000-726 Funchal, Portugal
    see more

  4. Museu de Electricidade - Casa da Luz

    The Museu de Electricidade - Casa da Luz (Electricity Museum - House of Light) is located in Funchal, Madeira, Portugal. Housed in a historic building that was once a power station, the museum is dedicated to the history of electricity and its role in the development of Madeira. It showcases the technological advancements in the region, from the first electricity generation to modern energy solutions. Exhibits include old machinery, interactive displays, and information about energy production and sustainable practices. The museum offers a fascinating look at how electricity has shaped both the island and the world.

    10:00-12:30,14:00-18:00 Recommended sightseeing time:1-2 hours Rua Casa da Luz, No.2, Funchal, Madeira, Portugal
    see more

  5. Sacred Art Museum of Funchal

    The Sacred Art Museum of Funchal (Museu de Arte Sacra do Funchal) is located in Funchal, Madeira, Portugal, and is housed in the Bishop's Palace (Paço Episcopal). The museum showcases a remarkable collection of religious art and artifacts from the 16th to 19th centuries, reflecting the island's rich Christian heritage. Highlights include paintings, sculptures, goldwork, and ecclesiastical vestments. The museum's collection emphasizes the influence of Portuguese baroque and Mannerist art, with works from renowned artists of the period. It provides an in-depth look at Madeira's religious history and is an important cultural destination for those interested in art and spirituality.

    10:00-17:30 Recommended sightseeing time:2-3 hours R. do Bpo. 21, 9000-073 Funchal, Portugal
    see more

  1. Madeira Botanical Garden

    The Madeira Botanical Garden (Jardim Botânico da Madeira) is located in Funchal, Madeira, Portugal, and is one of the island's most beautiful and well-preserved natural attractions. The garden spans over 35,000 square meters and features an impressive collection of native and exotic plant species, including a variety of tropical and subtropical plants. It is divided into different sections, such as medicinal plants, orchids, and cactus gardens, providing visitors with a diverse and vibrant experience. The garden offers stunning views of Funchal and the surrounding landscape, making it a peaceful spot for relaxation, nature walks, and photography.

    9:00-18:00 Recommended sightseeing time:1.5-3 hours 9060-345 Funchal, Portugal
    see more

  2. Monte Palace Madeira

    Monte Palace Madeira is a beautiful palace and garden complex located in the Monte area of Funchal, Madeira, Portugal. Originally built in the early 20th century as a private residence, the palace has been transformed into a museum and cultural center. The complex features exquisite gardens with exotic plants, koi ponds, and stunning views over Funchal. The museum inside the palace showcases a variety of art collections, including African art, Portuguese tilework, and contemporary sculptures. Visitors can also take a cable car from Funchal to Monte, offering a scenic ride up to the palace and gardens. It’s a must-visit destination for art lovers, history enthusiasts, and nature admirers.

    9:30-18:00 Recommended sightseeing time:2-3 hours Caminho das Babosas 4 e 4A, 9050-541 Funchal, Portugal
    see more

  3. Pico do Arieiro

    Pico do Arieiro is one of the highest peaks on the island of Madeira, Portugal, standing at 1,818 meters (5,998 feet) above sea level. It is known for its breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. The peak is accessible by car and offers a hiking trail that connects it to Pico Ruivo, the island’s highest point, providing an unforgettable experience for adventure seekers and nature lovers. Visitors can enjoy stunning views of rugged landscapes, cloud-covered valleys, and mountainous terrain. Pico do Arieiro is a popular spot for both tourists and locals looking to experience Madeira's dramatic scenery.

    24hours Recommended sightseeing time:2-4 hours E 202, Funchal, Madeira 9000-900, Portugal
    see more

  4. Ponta Gorda Lido

    Ponta Gorda Lido is a popular coastal swimming area located in Funchal, Madeira, Portugal. This lido features a seawater pool surrounded by beautiful rocky landscapes and views of the Atlantic Ocean. It is a perfect spot for swimming, sunbathing, and enjoying the natural beauty of Madeira's coastline. The area also includes restaurants, cafés, and sun loungers, providing a relaxing environment for visitors. Ponta Gorda Lido offers easy access to the sea, making it a great choice for those looking to enjoy the island’s refreshing waters in a calm, scenic setting.

    9:00-19:00 Recommended sightseeing time:1 hour Estrada Monumental 404, 9000-250 Funchal, Portugal
